== B4: Overflow and Near Overflow

=== 1.0 - Overview

==== Aharoni

Description: This model creates a test case for each of the following constraints on the intermediate result:

. All the numbers in the range [+MaxNorm - 3ulp, +MaxNorm + 3ulp]
. All the numbers in the range [−MaxNorm − 3 ulp, −MaxNorm + 3 ulp]
. A number that is larger than +MaxNorm + 3 ulp
. A number that is smaller than −MaxNorm − 3 ulp
. One number for every exponent in the range [MaxNorm.exp − 3, MaxNorm.exp + 3] for positive and negative numbers

Case i and ii are treated as one implementation group, with the sign axis looping over {0, 1}. Case iii and iv are likewise treated as one group.

*Number of Tests:* ~ 6 E 3

*Precisions Supported:* BF_16, FP_16, FP_32, FP_64, FP_128

*Operations Supported:* OP_ADD, OP_SUB, OP_MUL, OP_DIV, OP_FMADD, OP_FMSUB, OP_FNMADD, OP_FNMSUB

*Rounding Modes:* RNE, RTZ, RDN, RUP, RMM

*Enable Bits:* not available

NOTE: Operations such as OP_SQRT and pass-through operations like FSGNJ, FSGNJN, FSGNJX, FMIN, FMAX do not pass through the rounding pipeline and produce no pre-rounding intermediate, thus, they are excluded from B4.

==== 1.1 Test Case Organization

* *Group 1 – Aharoni Cases 1 & 2: The ±3 ULP Window*
** Intermediate constraint: The intermediate falls within [±MaxNorm − 3ulp, ±MaxNorm + 3ulp]
*** As this is the most sensitive region of the overflow boundary. The rounding mode determines whether the FPU clamps the result to MaxNorm or rounds up to ±Inf.
** *Sub-group 1A – T_k: integer ULP offsets*
*** Targets the FPU's overflow detection comparator. The intermediate is placed at the 7 integer-ULP spaced positions near the MaxNorm to exercise proper overflow detection.
** *Sub-group 1B - LGS: sub-ULP bit position*
*** Targets the rounding unit's internal logic. The intermediate is placed at each of the 7 combinations of the three bits {L, G, S} that the rounding units read to make its decision.

* *Group 2 – Aharoni Cases 3 & 4: Clear Overflow*
** Intermediate constraint: The intermediate is clearly above +MaxNorm + 3ulp or clearly below -MaxNorm - 3ulp
*** The result of this group is always in the unambiguous overflow region, resulting in ±Inf and the overflow flag is always raised regardless of rounding modes.

* *Group 3 – Aharoni Cases 5: Exponent Sweep*
** Intermediate constraint: One intermediate per exponent value in [MAXNORM_EXP − 3, MAXNORM_EXP + 3]
*** This group is not concerned with the mantissa, it covers the exponent neighborhood around the MaxNorm.
*** It confirms that the FPU handles exponent normalization and overflow detection correctly across the +/- 3 exponent band near MAXNORM_EXP.

==== 1.2 Goal of This Model

The B4 model tests three distinct hardware failure modes:

. *Incorrect overflow detection (Group 1A and 2)*
.. The FPU's comparator fails to correctly identify that the intermediate has exceeded MaxNorm.
... The T_k sub-group (Group 1A) places the intermediate at exact integer-ULP boundaries where overflow/no-overflow decisions are distinct.

. *Incorrect rounding unit logic (Group 1B)*
.. The FPU correctly detects the overflow region but computes {L,G,S} incorrectly or applies the wrong rounding logic.
... The LGS sub-group (Group 1B) places the intermediate at sub-ULP positions that directly exercise every combination of these three bits the rounding unit reads to ensure correct rounding logic is exhibited by the FPU.

. *Incorrect exponent handling near the boundary (Group 3)*
.. The FPU handles the right significand but applies the wrong exponent adjustments or normalization for intermediates in the +/- 3 exponent range of MaxNorm_exp. The exponent sweep covers this independently of the significand region.

==== 2.4 - Overlap between T_k and LGS sub-groups

As two sub-groups are created to cover Group 1 test cases, each designed to target different parts of the FPU hardware – rounding logic and overflow detection, we look at any overlaps between test cases and dismiss any repeated vectors.

* T_{0} = MaxNorm = {L, G, S} = {1, 0, 0} are identical intermediates. Both parameterizations place the intermediate at exactly MaxNorm with L = 1, G = 0, S = 0. This is the only true overlap between the two sub-groups, and it is emitted once and counted for both.
* T_{+1}, T_{+2}, T_{+3} are NOT in the LGS domain. There is no redundancy between T_{+1/+2/+3} and the LGS above-MaxNorm positions (LGS=101, 110, 111), and LGS=001/010/011 sit between T_{-1} and T_0, and LGS=101/110/111 sit between T_{0} and T_{+1}.
* T_{-1}, T_{-2}, T_{-3} have no LGS equivalents. These are exact representable values below MaxNorm with G=0 and S=0. The LGS=001, 010, 011 positions sit between T_{-1} and T_{0}.
* T_{+1}, T_{+2}, T_{+3} also contribute to the exp_range coverpoint (case v) alongside Group 3. This overlap is intentional as Sub-group 1A exercises those exponent bins through integer-ULP constructions targeting the overflow detection comparator, while Group 3 uses general overflow constructions with arbitrary mantissas.
=== 3.0 - Test Case Analysis

==== 3.1 Group 1: Case i & ii – the ±3 ULP Window

Goal: Drive the infinite-precision intermediate into the region [±MaxNorm − 3ulp, ±MaxNorm + 3ulp] using two complementary approaches that probe different layers of the FPU. The sign axis loops over {0, 1} for all sub-groups. Two sub-groups are used together because they probe different layers of the FPU:

* Sub-group 1A (T_k) targets the FPU's overflow detection comparator. The intermediate is placed at integer-ULP positions relative to MaxNorm.
* Sub-group 1B (LGS) targets the rounding unit's internal {L, G, S} logic. The intermediate is placed at sub-ULP positions that directly exercise every combination of the three bits the rounding units reads, including tie cases invisible to integer-ULP testing.

Operations covered across all Group 1 sub-groups:

* OP_ADD = A + B
* OP_SUB = A - B
* OP_MUL = A x B
* OP_DIV = A / B
* OP_FMADD = (A x B) + C
* OP_FMSUB = (A x B) - C
* OP_FNMADD = -(A x B) - C
* OP_FNMSUB = -(A x B) + C

===== 3.1.1: Sub-group 1A — T_k: Integer ULP Offsets

Goal: Drive the intermediate to each of the 7 values T_k for k ∈ {−3, −2, −1, 0, 1, 2, 3}, for both signs, under all 5 rounding modes, for each of the 8 operations.

We create vectors for 7 values T_k for k ∈ {−3..+3}, for both signs, under all 5 rounding modes, for each of the 8 operations — OP_ADD, OP_SUB, OP_FMADD, OP_FMSUB, OP_FNMADD, OP_FNMSUB.

Note: T_{+1}, T_{+2}, T_{+3} exceed MaxNorm and are not representable as input operands. Constructions use T_k/2 or MaxNorm + k·ulp via addition to produce the correct infinite-precision intermediate.

General Construction Method: (T_k)/2 is always representable. T_k itself is not representable for k > 0. Constructions are designed around (T_k)/2 as the primary operand where needed.

====== 3.1.1.1 | OP_ADD = A + B

Define:

* A = MaxNorm
* B = k·ulp (for k = 0, B = +0.0), k ∈ {−3..+3}

Then:

* Intermediate = A + B = MaxNorm + k·ulp = T_k (exact result, no rounding in operand encoding)

Note: B = k·ulp is always a small normal number within range for all formats and all k ∈ {−3..+3}. The addition is exact because B is k-ULP relative to A and lands in the guard/sticky region of the infinite-precision sum.

For sign = 1: negate A → A = −MaxNorm, intermediate = −T_k.

====== 3.1.1.2 | OP_SUB = A - B

Define:

* A = MaxNorm
* B = - k·ulp

Then:

* Intermediate = A - B = MaxNorm - (-k·ulp) = T_k

For k = 1: B = -ulp (negative) +
For k = -1: B = ulp (positive) +
For k = 0: B = 0

For sign = 1: negate A → intermediate = −T_k.

====== 3.1.1.3 | OP_MUL = A x B

Define:

* A = T_k / 2
* B = 2.0

Then:

* Intermediate = A x B = (T_k / 2) x 2 = T_k (exact, multiplication by 2 increments biased exponent by 1)

For sign = 1: negate A → A = −(T_k/2), intermediate = −T_k.

NOTE: (T_k)/2 is always representable and 2.0 is always representable.

====== 3.1.1.4 | OP_DIV = A / B

Define:

* A = T_k / 2
* B = 0.5

Then:

* Intermediate = A x B = (T_k / 2) / 0.5 = T_k (exact, division by 0.5 increments biased exponent by 1)

For sign = 1: negate A → A = −(T_k/2), intermediate = −T_k.

NOTE: we cannot use A = T_k, B = 1.0 for k > 0 because T_k is not representable for those cases.

====== 3.1.1.5 | OP_FMADD = (A x B) + C

Define:

* A = MaxNorm / 2
* B = 2.0
* C = k·ulp (for k = 0, C = +0.0)

Then:

* Intermediate = (MaxNorm/2 x 2.0) + k·ulp = MaxNorm + k·ulp = T_k

Product A x B = MaxNorm exactly. The fused addition of C = k·ulp gives T_k as the infinite-precision intermediate.

For sign = 1: negate A → product = −MaxNorm, intermediate = −MaxNorm + k·ulp = −T_{−k}. To target −T_k specifically, we can use k' = −k in C.

====== 3.1.1.6 | OP_FMSUB = (A x B) - C

Define:

* A = MaxNorm / 2
* B = 2.0
* C = - k·ulp (for k = 0, C = +0.0; for k = 1, C = −ulp; for k = −1, C = ulp)

Then:

* Intermediate = (MaxNorm/2 × 2.0) − (−k·ulp) = MaxNorm + k·ulp = T_k

For sign = 1: negate A → product = −MaxNorm, intermediate = −MaxNorm − (−k·ulp) = −MaxNorm + k·ulp = −T_{−k}.

====== 3.1.1.7 | OP_FNMADD = -(A x B) - C

To target positive T_k, we need a negative product:

* A = - (MaxNorm / 2)
* B = 2.0
* C = - k·ulp

Then:

* Intermediate = - ( -MaxNorm/2 x 2.0) − (−k·ulp) = MaxNorm + k·ulp = T_k

To target negative (-T_k), we need a positive product:

* A = MaxNorm / 2
* B = 2.0
* C = k·ulp

Then:

* Intermediate = −(MaxNorm/2 x 2.0) − k·ulp = −MaxNorm − k·ulp = −T_k

====== 3.1.1.8 | OP_FNMSUB = -(A x B) + C

To target positive T_k, we need a negative product:

* A = - (MaxNorm / 2)
* B = 2.0
* C = k·ulp

Then:

* Intermediate = - ( -MaxNorm/2 x 2.0) + (−k·ulp) = MaxNorm + k·ulp = T_k

To target negative (-T_k), we need a positive product:

* A = MaxNorm / 2
* B = 2.0
* C = - k·ulp

Then:

* Intermediate = −(MaxNorm/2 x 2.0) − k·ulp = −MaxNorm − k·ulp = −T_k

*Total Test Cases for Group 1A:* 7 values of k x 2 signs x 5 rounding modes x 8 operations = *560 per format*

===== 3.1.2: Sub-group 1B (Arithmetic) — LGS: Sub-ULP Bit Positions

Goal: Produce an intermediate where the three bits {L, G, S} have specific values.

This subgroup exercises all 7 non-zero LGS bins at the MaxNorm exponent boundary with the 5 rounding modes and 6 arithmetic operations — OP_ADD, OP_SUB, OP_FMADD, OP_FMSUB, OP_FNMADD, OP_FNMSUB.

(OP_MUL and OP_DIV are handled separately in Sub-group 1B (MUL/DIV) because they cannot independently set G and S bits. See section 3.1.3.)

*General Construction Method:*

[cols="1,1,1,1,1,1,1,2",options="header"]
|===
| Config | use_m1ulp | LGS sample | L | G | S | gs | Position

| 1 | True  | 001 | 0 | 0 | 1 | 1 | MaxNorm - 1ulp +¼ ulp
| 2 | True  | 010 | 0 | 1 | 0 | 2 | MaxNorm - 1ulp +½ ulp
| 3 | True  | 011 | 0 | 1 | 1 | 3 | MaxNorm - 1ulp +¾ ulp
| 4 | False | 100 | 1 | 0 | 0 | 0 | Exactly MaxNorm
| 5 | False | 101 | 1 | 0 | 1 | 1 | MaxNorm +¼ ulp
| 6 | False | 110 | 1 | 1 | 0 | 2 | MaxNorm +½ ulp
| 7 | False | 111 | 1 | 1 | 1 | 3 | MaxNorm +¾ ulp
|===

gs = A 2-bit integer ∈ {0, 1, 2, 3} encoding the desired G and S values: +
E.g. gs bit 1 → G, gs bit 0 → S, and L is controlled separately through A operand selection. B = gs x 2^(sub_ulp_exp).

For each of the 7 LGS configurations, define:

* A_lgs = MaxNorm if use_m1ulp = False, or MaxNorm−1ulp if use_m1ulp = True
* B_lgs = _gs_b (gs, sign, E, M, bias)

*A Operand selection (A_lgs):* A sets the {L} bit.

MaxNorm has mantissa = all ones = 111…111 (M ones). Its LSB is 1, so L = 1. +
MaxNorm - 1ulp has mantissa = all ones minus one = 111…110 (M-1 ones, then a zero). Its LSB is 0 so L = 0.

We use the use_m1ulp method to set the L bit:

* use_m1ulp = False
** A = MaxNorm -> mantissa = all ones
** L = 1
* use_m1ulp = True
** A = MaxNorm - 1ulp -> mantissa = all ones with LSB cleared
** L = 0

*B Operand construction (B_lgs):* B sets the {G} and {S} bit.

B is constructed as: B = gs x 2^(sub_ulp_exp), where sub_ulp_exp = ulp_exp - 2.

This works by tracing where B's bits land when it is added with A. Recall that value of MaxNorm is: 1.111…111 x 2^(unbiased_max).

gs is a 2-bit integer where bit 1 encodes G and bit 0 encodes S directly. B is a sub-ULP value — smaller than 1 ULP at MaxNorm's magnitude — so when added to A it does not shift A's exponent. Its bits land exactly in the G and S positions of the intermediate:

----
gs in binary:  bit1  bit0
                ↓     ↓
Multiply by 2^(ulp_exp - 2):
  gs bit 1  →  value 2^(ulp_exp - 1)  →  G position  ✓
  gs bit 0  →  value 2^(ulp_exp - 2)  →  S position  ✓
----

Therefore: {L, G, S} = {A_mantissa_LSB, gs bit 1, gs bit 0}

*Example for F16, LGS=110, OP_ADD, sign = 0:*

Define the format parameters:

----
E = 5,   M = 10,   bias = 15
max_biased   = (1 << 5) - 2  = 30
unbiased_max = 30 - 15       = 15
ulp_exp      = 15 - 10       = 5    →  1 ULP at MaxNorm = 2^5 = 32
sub_exp      = 5 - 2         = 3    →  sub_ulp = 2^3 = 8
----

This means:

* L = 1 -> use A = MaxNorm
* G = 1, S = 0 -> gs = 2 (binary 10, bit 1 = 1, bit 0 = 0)

====== 3.1.2.1 | OP_ADD = A + B

Positive intermediate +(A + B):

* A = +A_lgs, B = +B_lgs
* Intermediate = A_lgs + B_lgs = +(A + B)

Negative intermediate −(A + B):

* A = −A_lgs, B = −B_lgs
* Intermediate = −A_lgs + (−B_lgs) = −(A + B)

Note: The addition is exact in infinite precision because B_lgs is sub-ULP relative to A_lgs. B's bits land in the G and S positions without shifting A's exponent. The guard condition is satisfied by construction since both MaxNorm and MaxNorm−1ulp have M−1 leading ones in the mantissa.

====== 3.1.2.2 | OP_SUB = A − B

Positive intermediate +(A + B):

* A = +A_lgs, B = −B_lgs
* Intermediate = A_lgs − (−B_lgs) = A_lgs + B_lgs = +(A + B)

Negative intermediate −(A + B):

* A = −A_lgs, B = +B_lgs
* Intermediate = −A_lgs − B_lgs = −(A + B)

====== 3.1.2.3 | OP_FMADD = (A x B) + C

FMADD uses 1.0 as the multiplier so the product equals A_lgs exactly. C plays the role of the sub-ULP addend.

Positive intermediate +(A + B):

* A = +A_lgs, B = 1.0, C = +B_lgs
* Intermediate = (A_lgs × 1.0) + B_lgs = +(A + B)

Negative intermediate −(A + B):

* A = −A_lgs, B = 1.0, C = −B_lgs
* Intermediate = (−A_lgs × 1.0) + (−B_lgs) = −(A + B)

====== 3.1.2.4 | OP_FMSUB = (A x B) − C

Positive intermediate +(A + B):

* A = +A_lgs, B = 1.0, C = −B_lgs
* Intermediate = (A_lgs × 1.0) − (−B_lgs) = A_lgs + B_lgs = +(A + B)

Negative intermediate −(A + B):

* A = −A_lgs, B = 1.0, C = +B_lgs
* Intermediate = (−A_lgs × 1.0) − B_lgs = −(A + B)

====== 3.1.2.5 | OP_FNMADD = -(A x B) - C

A negative product is required to reach positive T_k without C needing to equal 2·MaxNorm (which is not representable).

Positive intermediate +(A + B):

* A = −A_lgs, B = 1.0, C = −B_lgs
* Intermediate = −(−A_lgs × 1.0) − (−B_lgs) = A_lgs + B_lgs = +(A + B)

Negative intermediate −(A + B):

* A = +A_lgs, B = 1.0, C = +B_lgs
* Intermediate = −(A_lgs × 1.0) − B_lgs = −(A + B)

====== 3.1.2.6 | OP_FNMSUB = -(A x B) + C

Positive intermediate +(A + B):

* A = −A_lgs, B = 1.0, C = +B_lgs
* Intermediate = −(−A_lgs × 1.0) + B_lgs = A_lgs + B_lgs = +(A + B)

Negative intermediate −(A + B):

* A = +A_lgs, B = 1.0, C = −B_lgs
* Intermediate = −(A_lgs × 1.0) + (−B_lgs) = −(A + B)

*Total Test Cases for Group 1B (Arithmetic):* 7 LGS configs x 2 signs x 5 rounding modes x 6 operations = *420 per format*

===== 3.1.3: Sub-group 1B (MUL/DIV Supplement) — LGS=100 Only

Goal: Cover the op_mul and op_div bins in the covergroup cross product at the MaxNorm exponent boundary.

OP_MUL and OP_DIV cannot independently set the G and S bits of the intermediate. They are restricted to LGS=100 (exact MaxNorm) only and are handled in a dedicated supplement loop separate from the main LGS arithmetic loop.

Why MUL and DIV are separated from the main LGS loop:

The main LGS loop works because ADD, SUB, and the FMA family can structure the intermediate as A + B, where a tiny sub-ULP value B independently populates G and S without disturbing A's exponent. MUL and DIV do not have this structural freedom:

* For OP_MUL: the only multiplier that keeps the intermediate at MAXNORM_EXP without shifting the exponent is 1.0. Any other multiplier either shifts the exponent out of the MaxNorm band or produces a product whose guard and sticky bits cannot be cleanly targeted.
* For OP_DIV: the same constraint applies. Only a divisor of 1.0 preserves the exponent and produces G = 0, S = 0.

The result in both cases is always LGS=100 — exact MaxNorm with no rounding bits. LGS=100 is already covered by config (False, gs=0) in the main arithmetic loop. This supplement does not add new LGS coverage. Its sole purpose is to satisfy the op_mul and op_div bins in the covergroup cross product.

====== 3.1.3.1 | OP_MUL = A x B

Define:

* A = MaxNorm (sign = 0 or 1)
* B = 1.0

Then:

* Intermediate = MaxNorm x 1.0 = MaxNorm -> LGS = 100 (G = 0, S = 0)

For sign = 1: A = −MaxNorm, intermediate = −MaxNorm.

====== 3.1.3.2 | OP_DIV = A / B

Define:

* A = MaxNorm (sign = 0 or 1)
* B = 1.0

Then:

* Intermediate = MaxNorm / 1.0 = MaxNorm -> LGS = 100 (G = 0, S = 0)

For sign = 1: A = −MaxNorm, intermediate = −MaxNorm.

*Total Test Cases for Group 1B (MUL/DIV):* 1 LGS configs x 2 signs x 5 rounding modes x 2 operations = *20 per format*

==== 3.2 Group 2: Case iii & iv – clear overflow

Goal: Drive the intermediate clearly above MaxNorm + 3 ulp (positive) or below −MaxNorm − 3ulp (negative). One construction per operation per sign. The result is always ±Inf and the overflow flag is always raised regardless of rounding mode.

Operand construction per operation: All constructions target 2·MaxNorm as the intermediate, which is well above MaxNorm + 3 ulp for all formats.

|===
| Operation | A | B | C | Intermediate

| OP_ADD    | MaxNorm    | MaxNorm  | N/A      | 2·MaxNorm
| OP_SUB    | MaxNorm    | -MaxNorm | N/A      | 2·MaxNorm
| OP_MUL    | MaxNorm    | 2.0      | N/A      | 2·MaxNorm
| OP_DIV    | MaxNorm    | 0.5      | N/A      | 2·MaxNorm
| OP_FMADD  | MaxNorm/2  | 2.0      | MaxNorm  | 2·MaxNorm
| OP_FMSUB  | MaxNorm/2  | 2.0      | -MaxNorm | 2·MaxNorm

For sign = 1: negate the dominant operand in each construction to target −2·MaxNorm.

==== 3.3 Group 3: Case v – the exponent sweep

Goal: Exercise one intermediate per exponent value in [max_biased − 3, max_biased + 3], for both signs, all rounding modes, all 8 operations. The mantissa value is not constrained — only the exponent matters.

Define d = target biased exponent - max_biased, thus d ∈ {−3, −2, −1, 0, +1, +2, +3}.

===== 3.3.1 | Case 1: d ≤ 0 (exponents at or below MaxNorm.exp)

A normal number at biased exponent max_biased + d exists and is representable. Construct A with that biased exponent and mantissa of all-ones. Then, we use a neutral second operand to pass the intermediate through with the exponent unchanged.

[cols="1,1,1,1",options="header"]
|===
| Operation | A | B | C

| OP_ADD    | A  | 0   | N/A
| OP_SUB    | A  | 0   | N/A
| OP_MUL    | A  | 1.0 | N/A
| OP_DIV    | A  | 1.0 | N/A
| OP_FMADD  | A  | 1.0 | 0
| OP_FMSUB  | A  | 1.0 | 0
| OP_FNMADD | -A | 1.0 | 0
| OP_FNSUB  | -A | 1.0 | 0
|===

For sign = 1: negate A in each construction.

===== 3.3.2 | Case 2: d > 0 (exponents above MaxNorm.exp)

No representable finite number has a biased exponent above max_biased. The intermediate must be produced via overflow arithmetic. The intermediate exponent will be max_biased + d.

The canonical construction is FMUL(MaxNorm, 2^d). For the other operations, equivalent constructions are used:

- B is a floating point value where its biased exp = max_biased + d - 1

[cols="1,1,1,1",options="header"]
|===
| Operation | A | B | C

| OP_ADD    | MaxNorm  | B      | N/A
| OP_SUB    | MaxNorm  | B      | N/A
| OP_MUL    | MaxNorm  | 2^d    | N/A
| OP_DIV    | MaxNorm  | 2^(-d) | N/A
| OP_FMADD  | MaxNorm  | 2^d    | 0
| OP_FMSUB  | MaxNorm  | 2^d    | 0
| OP_FNMADD | -MaxNorm | 2^d    | 0
| OP_FNSUB  | -MaxNorm | 2^d    | 0
|===

For sign = 1: negate the dominant operand.

*Total Test Cases for Group 3:* 7 exponents x 2 signs x 5 rounding modes x 8 operations = *560 per format*
